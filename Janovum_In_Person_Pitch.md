# JANOVUM — IN-PERSON SALES PITCH GUIDE

> This isn't a word-for-word script. It's a framework. Study it, internalize it, and make it yours. The key to in-person pitching is being natural — you're having a conversation, not reading a teleprompter.

---

## THE MINDSET

Before you even open your mouth, remember:
- You're not selling. You're **solving a problem** they already have.
- You're not begging for their business. You're **offering something valuable**.
- You're not a random kid. You're the **founder of an AI company** that can save them thousands.
- Confidence comes from knowing your product inside and out. Study this until you can explain it in your sleep.

---

## PHASE 1: THE WALK-IN

### Where to go
- Local businesses that answer phones: restaurants, salons, barbershops, dental offices, law firms, real estate offices, auto shops, medical clinics, gyms, cleaning companies, contractors, spas
- Look for businesses that are **busy** — if they're busy, they're missing calls
- Look for businesses with a **"Now Hiring"** sign — they need help and can't find it
- Look for businesses where the owner is **working the front desk** — they're stretched thin

### First impression
- Dress clean and professional but not overdressed — business casual
- Walk in with confidence, not hesitation
- Don't look at your phone. Make eye contact.
- Have a business card ready (or your phone with janovum.com pulled up)

### The approach

Walk up to whoever's at the front. Be friendly, not salesy.

**"Hey, how's it going? Is the owner around by any chance?"**

If they ARE the owner:
**"Nice to meet you — I'm Jaden, I run a company called Janovum. I'm not gonna take up a lot of your time, I was actually just walking by and wanted to ask you something real quick."**

If the owner isn't there:
**"No worries — when's a good time to catch them? And what's their name?"**
*(Write it down. Come back at that time. Now you have a warm intro: "Hey [name], [front desk person] said I should come talk to you.")*

---

## PHASE 2: THE CONVERSATION

### The opening question

Don't pitch yet. Ask a question first. This does two things: (1) shows you care about their business, and (2) tells you exactly what to pitch.

**"So I'm curious — how do you guys handle your phone calls right now? Like when you're busy with a customer and the phone rings, what happens?"**

OR (depending on the business):

**"When someone calls after hours or on the weekend, what happens to that call?"**

OR:

**"How are you guys getting new customers right now? Is it mostly word of mouth, or are you doing online stuff too?"**

*(Let them talk. Listen. Whatever pain point they mention — that's your angle.)*

---

### Common answers and how to respond

**"We just let it go to voicemail"**
→ "Yeah, that's super common. The thing is, studies show like 80% of people who hit voicemail never leave a message — they just call the next place. So every missed call is potentially a customer going to your competitor."

**"I answer it myself"**
→ "That's what a lot of owners do, but then you're getting pulled away from the customer standing right in front of you. And that person notices. It's like you can't win either way."

**"We have someone at the front desk"**
→ "That's solid. But what about after hours? Weekends? When they call in sick? That's where most businesses leak leads without even knowing it."

**"We don't really get that many calls"**
→ "That might actually be the problem — if people are calling and not getting through, they're not calling back. You wouldn't even know you're missing them. We actually have businesses that doubled their appointments just by answering every call."

## PHASE 3: THE PITCH

Once they've told you their problem, NOW you pitch — and you pitch directly to what they said.

### The core pitch (customize based on their answer)

**"Yeah, so that's exactly what my company does. We set up AI systems for businesses — the main one is an AI receptionist. Basically, someone calls your number, and instead of voicemail or nobody picking up, an AI answers. And it doesn't sound like a robot — it sounds like a real person at the front desk. It knows your services, your prices, your hours, everything. It can book appointments, answer questions, take messages, even route calls to you if it's urgent. And it runs 24/7 — nights, weekends, holidays."**

### Make it real for them

Connect it to THEIR business specifically. Use their name, their industry:

**"So like for you guys, imagine someone calls at 8 PM wanting to book [their service]. Right now that call goes nowhere. With us, the AI picks up, has a conversation with them, books the appointment, and you wake up the next morning with a new customer on your schedule. You didn't do anything."**

### The dashboard

**"And you get a full dashboard where you can see every call — who called, what they said, what the AI told them, any appointments it booked. All in real time on your phone. So you always know what's going on."**

## PHASE 4: THE OBJECTIONS

### "How much does it cost?"

Don't dodge it. Be straightforward.

**"So it's a thousand to get everything set up — that covers the AI getting trained on your business, your own phone number, the dashboard, everything. And then it's $500 a month after that. No hidden fees, no per-minute charges."**

**"And just to put it in perspective — a part-time receptionist costs you what, $15-$20 an hour? That's $2,500-$3,000 a month minimum. Our AI does the same thing 24/7 for $500. And it never calls in sick."**

### "That sounds expensive"

**"I get it. But think about it this way — how much is one new customer worth to you? [Wait for answer]. So if the AI books even one or two extra appointments a month that you would've missed, it literally pays for itself. Everything after that is profit."**

### "I don't know about AI"

**"I totally understand. A lot of people think robot voice, right? But it's actually nothing like that. It sounds like a regular person picking up the phone. Most callers have no idea. And you can listen to every single call — if you don't like something, we change it same day."**

### "Let me think about it"

**"For sure, no rush. Here—"** *(hand them your card or show them your phone)* **"check out janovum.com — there's an FAQ section that covers everything. And if you ever want to see it in action, I can do a quick demo over Google Meet. Takes 15 minutes."**

### "We're doing fine without it"

**"That's good to hear. But let me ask you — do you know how many calls you're missing after hours or when you're busy? Most businesses don't, because the calls just... stop. They go somewhere else. We've had clients who thought they were doing fine and then found out they were losing 30-40% of their incoming calls."**

### "I need to talk to my partner/manager"

**"Makes total sense. Tell you what — here's my info. And if it helps, I can hop on a quick call with both of you. Sometimes it's easier when everyone hears it at the same time."**

---

## PHASE 5: THE CLOSE

You're not trying to sign them on the spot (although if they're ready, go for it). The goal is to get one of these:

### Best case: Book the demo right there
**"Look, the easiest way to see how it works is a quick 15-minute demo. I can show you your AI receptionist in action — what it sounds like, how it books appointments, everything. What day works for you this week?"**

*(Pull out your phone, open the calendar, and book it right there. Send them the Google Meet link on the spot.)*

### Good case: Get their contact info
**"Let me send you some info — what's the best email? I'll shoot you a link where you can see everything and book a time that works for you."**

*(Send them janovum.com/faq + follow up in 2 days)*

### Minimum: Leave your info
**"Here's my card — check out janovum.com when you get a chance. And seriously, if you ever want to see it, I'm 15 minutes away. No pressure."**

---

## PHASE 6: THE FOLLOW-UP

This is where most people drop the ball. The fortune is in the follow-up.

### If you got their email:
- **Day 0:** Send a quick email: "Great meeting you today at [business]. Here's the FAQ I mentioned: janovum.com/faq. Let me know if you want to see a demo — happy to set one up."
- **Day 2:** Follow up: "Hey [name], just checking in — did you get a chance to look at that link? Happy to answer any questions."
- **Day 5:** One more: "Hey [name], last thing — I set up a quick 15-min demo slot this [day]. No commitment, just wanted to show you how it works. Here's the link to book: [booking link]"
- **Day 10+:** If no response, move on. Some people come back weeks or months later.

### If you only left a card:
- Stop back in 3-5 days: **"Hey, I came by last week — just wanted to see if you had any questions about what we talked about."**

## STUDY PLAN

1. **Read this whole thing 3 times.** Not skimming — actually read it.
2. **Practice the opening** in the mirror until it sounds natural, not rehearsed.
3. **Memorize the numbers** — pricing, setup time, what's included. You should be able to rattle these off without thinking.
4. **Practice objection handling** — have a friend throw random objections at you and respond naturally.
5. **Role play the full conversation** from walk-in to close. Record yourself and listen back.
6. **Go do 3 walk-ins a day minimum.** The first few will be rough. By day 5 you'll be smooth. By day 10 you'll be closing.
7. **After every interaction, write down:** What went well? What could be better? What did they ask that you weren't ready for?

## REMEMBER

- The first 10 seconds decide everything. Be warm, confident, and direct.
- You're not asking for a favor. You're offering a solution.
- Every "no" gets you closer to a "yes."
- The business owners who say no today might say yes in 3 months when they're drowning in missed calls.
- You built this company. You know this product better than anyone. Own it.
