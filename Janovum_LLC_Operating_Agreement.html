Operating Agreement

of Janovum LLC, a Florida Limited Liability Company

Effective Date: _______________, 2026

This Operating Agreement ("Agreement") is made and entered into as of the date set forth above by the undersigned Member of Janovum LLC ("the Company"), a limited liability company organized under the laws of the State of Florida.

Article I — Formation

1.1   The Company was formed by the filing of Articles of Organization with the Florida Department of State, Division of Corporations, in accordance with the Florida Revised Limited Liability Company Act (Chapter 605, Florida Statutes).

1.2   The name of the Company is Janovum LLC.

1.3   The principal place of business shall be at the Member's residence or such other location as the Member may designate from time to time.

1.4   The registered agent for service of process is Jaden Gonzalez, at the principal place of business address.

Article II — Purpose

2.1   The purpose of the Company is to engage in technology services, artificial intelligence consulting, AI system deployment and maintenance, and any other lawful business activity permitted under Florida law.

Article III — Term

3.1   The Company shall exist in perpetuity unless dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or as required by law.

Article IV — Membership

4.1   The Company is a single-member LLC. The sole Member is:

Jaden Gonzalez
Ownership Interest: 100%
Capital Contribution: $500.00 (initial working capital)

4.2   The Member shall not be personally liable for any debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being a Member.

Article V — Management & Decision Making

5.1   The Company shall be member-managed. The sole Member shall have full and exclusive authority to manage the business and affairs of the Company.

5.2   The Member shall have the authority to:

  1. Enter into contracts and agreements on behalf of the Company;
  2. Open and manage bank accounts;
  3. Hire employees, contractors, and agents;
  4. Purchase, lease, or sell assets;
  5. Borrow funds or extend credit on behalf of the Company;
  6. Make all operational and strategic decisions.

Article VI — Capital Contributions

6.1   The initial capital contribution of the Member is $500.00.

6.2   Additional capital contributions may be made at the Member's sole discretion. No Member shall be required to make additional contributions.

Article VII — Profits, Losses & Distributions

7.1   All profits and losses of the Company shall be allocated entirely to the sole Member.

7.2   Distributions of available cash shall be made at the sole discretion of the Member, at such times and in such amounts as the Member determines, provided that the Company retains sufficient funds to meet its obligations.

7.3   As a single-member LLC, the Company shall be treated as a disregarded entity for federal income tax purposes. All income, deductions, and credits shall be reported on the Member's personal tax return (Schedule C, Form 1040).

Article VIII — Adding New Members & Equity

8.1   New members may only be admitted with the written consent of the existing Member(s).

8.2   Upon admission of any new member, this Agreement shall be amended in writing to reflect the new ownership percentages, capital contributions, and profit/loss allocations.

8.3   Any transfer, sale, or assignment of membership interest requires the prior written consent of all existing Members.

8.4   If equity is offered to investors or partners in the future, the terms shall be documented in a separate written agreement (e.g., equity purchase agreement or subscription agreement) and this Operating Agreement shall be updated accordingly.

Article IX — Removal of Members

9.1   A member may voluntarily withdraw from the Company by providing 60 days' written notice.

9.2   Upon withdrawal or removal, the departing member's interest shall be valued at fair market value as determined by mutual agreement or, failing that, by an independent appraiser.

9.3   The Company or remaining members shall have the right of first refusal to purchase the departing member's interest before it may be offered to any third party.

Article X — Sale of the Company

10.1   The Company may be sold in whole or in part upon the approval of Members holding a majority of the ownership interests.

10.2   In the event of a sale, merger, or acquisition, proceeds shall be distributed to Members in proportion to their ownership percentages after settling all Company debts and obligations.

Article XI — Dissolution

11.1   The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following:

  1. The written decision of the Member(s) holding a majority interest;
  2. The sale of all or substantially all of the Company's assets;
  3. Any event that makes it unlawful to continue the Company's business;
  4. A judicial decree of dissolution.

11.2   Upon dissolution, the Company's assets shall be distributed in the following order:

  1. Payment of all debts and liabilities to creditors;
  2. Payment of any outstanding obligations to Members (loans, unpaid distributions);
  3. Distribution of remaining assets to Members in proportion to ownership percentages.

Article XII — Indemnification

12.1   The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Member, managers, employees, and agents from any claims, damages, or expenses arising from their good-faith actions on behalf of the Company, except in cases of willful misconduct or gross negligence.

Article XIII — Amendments

13.1   This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by Members holding a majority of the ownership interests.

Article XIV — Governing Law

14.1   This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Florida.

Article XV — Severability

15.1   If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the undersigned Member has executed this Operating Agreement as of the date first written above.

Jaden Gonzalez — Sole Member & Manager
